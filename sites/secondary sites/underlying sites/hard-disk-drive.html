HDD - Hard Disk Drive
− An HDD or Hard Disk Drive is a storage device that uses magnetic storage to store and retrieve digital information through rapidly rotating disks.

What is a HHD what does it do?

A HDD is a non-volatile data storage device that stores and retrieves digital information using magnetic storage and rapidly rotating disks coated in magnetic material. The data is accessed using read/write heads that float above the spinning platters. HDDs are a common type of storage in computers and other electronic devices because they offer high-capacity storage at a relatively low cost.

How the HHD works

When you save data on an HDD, the information is written to the magnetic coating on the platters in the form of binary code. When you access data, the read/write heads locate the specific track and sector on the disk where the data is stored. Data is read or written by changing the magnetic orientation of tiny regions on the platters surface.

Advatages of HDD

1. High Capacity: HDDs are available in large storage capacities, making them suitable for storing big amounts of data. For example: documents, photos, videos and software applications.

2. Cost-Effective: HDDs offer a cost-effective solution for storage, especially for larger storage capacities compared to alternatives like solid-state drives (SSDs).

Disadvatages of HDD

1. Slower Speed: HDDs are slower than SSDs in terms of data access and transfer speeds because they rely on mechanical parts and spinning disks.

2. Vulnerable to Physical Damage: Since HDDs have moving parts, they are more susceptible to damage from physical shock or movement compared to SSDs, which have no moving parts.

Having a sufficient HDD

Evaluate your storage requirements, ensur that the drive's capacity is sufficient for your data needs. Take into account the rotational speed, where higher values result in quicker data access. Determine the drive's intended purpose; different tasks require varying levels of performance, whether it's basic storage, gaming, or professional applications. You should also consider noise levels, especially for setups where quiet operation is important.
