Other PC-components
− PCs are extremly complex and are buildt up of many different components, here are some of the components that we didn't elaborate on earlier

Network Cards

− A network card, also known as a network adapter is a hardware component that enables computers to connect to a network, facilitating data exchange and communication with other devices

Generally about Network Cards:

A network card is an essential part of modern computers and enables communication between devices in a network by converting data from the computer into a format that can be transmitted over the network, and vice versa. Network cards can be integrated into the motherboard of a computer or installed as a separate expansion card. They support various networking technologies such as Ethernet, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth, allowing computers to access local area networks or connect to the internet. Network cards have unique addresses that identify them on a network, facilitating the exchange of data between devices and enabling internet connectivity.

PSU - Power Supply Unit

− The PSU converts electrical power from an outlet into usable power for the computer components. It supplies power to the motherboard, CPU, and other components

Generally about the PSU:

The PSU is a critical component within a computer, responsible for converting electrical power from an outlet into the appropriate voltages and currents needed to power the various internal components. Essentially acting as the computer's energy hub, the PSU ensures a stable and reliable power supply to the motherboard, CPU, graphics card, storage devices, and other computer components. It comes in various wattages and often includes multiple connectors to accommodate different types of devices. Modern PSUs also have safety features such as overvoltage and overcurrent protection, safeguarding the computer from potential electrical issues. Efficient and dependable power delivery is essential for the smooth operation, making the PSU a fundamental element in every computer setup.

Fans - Cooling Systems

− Fans in a PC dissipate heat by generating airflow, preventing overheating and ensuring optimal performance of internal components.

Generally about Fans:

Fans in computers are essential components responsible for cooling internal hardware and maintaining optimal operating temperatures. By generating airflow, they dissipate heat generated by the CPU, GPU, and other components, preventing overheating and potential damage. Fans are strategically placed within the computer case and often on specific components like the CPU heatsink and graphics card, to draw in cool air and expel hot air outside. Modern computer fans come with variable speeds, allowing them to adjust their rotation based on the computers temperature, which also contributes to minimal noise. Efficient cooling ensures the stable performance of the computer and prolongs the lifespan of critical components.
